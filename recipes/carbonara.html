Carbonara

Carbonara is a creamy pasta with eggs, cheese, and meat.

Carbonara is a classic Italian pasta dish made with eggs, grated cheese (usually Parmesan or Pecorino Romano), black pepper, and pancetta or beef bacon. The heat from the pasta creates a rich, creamy sauce without using cream.

Ingredients

  • 400g spaghetti
  • 150g pancetta or beef bacon
  • 2 large eggs
  • 100g grated Parmesan cheese
  • Salt and black pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Cook the spaghetti in salted boiling water until al dente.
  2. In a pan, cook the pancetta or beef bacon until crispy.
  3. In a bowl, whisk together the eggs and grated cheese.
  4. Drain the pasta and add it to the pan with the pancetta.
  5. Remove from heat and quickly mix in the egg and cheese mixture.
  6. Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  7. Serve immediately with extra cheese on top.
